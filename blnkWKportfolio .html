[blnk]
MAGAZINE

The independent lifestyle magazine
for progressive creative thinking.

[blnk] is a magazine that bravely tests out the limits of the existing human being.

Its purpose is to encourage its readers to engage in creative and inspiring actions. With its overall bold style [blnk] motivates people to thinking and especially acting out of the box.

"To be inspiring and to be inspired" is the main message [blnk] is made to deliver to its readers because we believe that individuality and creativity is what makes the world go round.

Its main target group are young creators that want to make a positive impact on the world and for the future of society.

That is why there are several chapters consisting of mainly interviews with fellow creative people, who have found their own unique way of contributing to this society.

The categories are:
[architecture]
[art]
[design]
[fashion] and
[society].

Setting the focus on the development of the individual and positive change in society, [blnk] suggest the possibility of a utopian future while rising awareness on its possible dystopian counterpart, as an article featuring the fictional 'Sibyl System' describes.

The magazine comes to life through its strong visual language and expressive photos that are used to communicate with the reader. Especially its chapters featuring artists and their art are presented with many full page pictures. Is that not a way to leave an imprint on people's minds?

Like that [blnk] fills the gap where inspiration for strong and young minds is desperately needed with loud and genuine passion. Communication through pictures is especially essential for our modern fast-living world and lifestyle.

To capture the eye and also mind of the reader, pictures and typography are frequently used to create a loud composition and therefore urgent way of communication.

To visualize different subjects in their own unique way, the magazine utilizes greyscale images as well. Combinations with colorful and color-coordinated typography are used in different sizes never shying away from crossing an image or just becoming a stylistic device for its aesthetics.

While [blnk] seems to combine a lot of different styles of presenting its chapters, each difference contributes to the magazine's looks as a whole, making it harmonize in its contrasts.

Individuality is what makes the world go round. The article about japanese artist and designer Hiroto Ikeuchi presents cybernetic fashion and funny enough face masks, which is as contemporary as it can get. Loud and uniquely this leads to different topics like modernism and tradition, as well as the ever-present question for sustainability.

This magazine was created as a fictional university project,
together with Celina Hofmann and Michaela Kappes.
